CGC Grading & Submission Channels

CGC (Certified Guaranty Company) grading has transformed the comic book market — high-grade slabbed books command massive premiums over raw copies, and understanding the grading process is essential for serious collectors. CGC-focused comic channels cover submission strategy (what to grade, what tier to use, how to press), grading standards (what separates a 9.6 from a 9.8), and how to read CGC census data to identify valuable low-population books.

CGC grading tip from top creators: Focus grading submissions on books where the graded premium exceeds the cost of grading. Key first appearances, death issues, and low-print runs tied to MCU announcements tend to return the best grading ROI. Most creators recommend the Economy tier for non-urgent submissions to keep costs manageable.

Spec & Modern Comics Creators

Spec collecting — buying modern comics that might spike in value based on MCU/DCEU casting news, show announcements, or first appearances becoming relevant — has its own thriving creator community. The best spec channels combine market awareness with genuine comic knowledge, helping collectors identify books worth holding vs. books to flip quickly when news hits. ComicTom101 is the most influential spec voice, but a growing community of spec-focused Discord communities and YouTube channels provides complementary perspectives.

Golden Age & Silver Age Comic Collectors

Golden Age (1938–1956) and Silver Age (1956–1970) comic collecting is a different world from modern spec — it requires deep historical knowledge, careful authentication, and patience. The best vintage comic channels cover how to identify key issues, how to evaluate restoration, what grades are realistic for Golden Age books, and how to source vintage comics at fair prices through auctions, dealers, and estate sales.

Original Comic Art Collectors

Original comic art — the actual hand-drawn pages, covers, and splash pages used to produce published comics — is one of the most exciting and underserved corners of the hobby. Original art doesn't have a standardized pricing guide, making creator knowledge especially valuable. Channels covering original art help collectors understand provenance, how to authenticate pieces, what makes a page desirable (cover vs. splash vs. interior page), and how to navigate the ComicArtFans and Heritage auction markets.
