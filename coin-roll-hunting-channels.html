Coin roll hunting (CRH) is one of the most accessible and addictive corners of the coin collecting hobby — you get rolls of coins from the bank, search through them for silver coins, key dates, and errors, then return the non-keepers. YouTube CRH channels document these searches in real time, teaching you exactly what to look for while making for genuinely exciting viewing. This guide covers the best YouTube channels for coin roll hunting in 2025.

What Makes a Great Coin Roll Hunting YouTube Channel?

The best CRH channels combine entertainment with genuine education. You should be able to watch a channel and walk away knowing exactly how to identify the coins they find, what makes them valuable, and how to replicate their search strategy. Channels that only show big finds without explaining what to look for are entertainment — channels that teach you why something is valuable are genuinely educational.

What to Look for When Coin Roll Hunting

The best CRH YouTube channels will teach you all of these, but here's a quick reference for what makes a coin worth keeping:

  • Silver Roosevelt Dimes (1946–1964): 90% silver. Currently worth ~15–20x face value. Look for the date — any dime dated 1964 or earlier is silver
  • Silver Washington Quarters (pre-1965): 90% silver. Currently worth $4–5+ each as silver. Same rule — 1964 or earlier = silver
  • War Nickels (1942–1945): 35% silver. Identifiable by the large mint mark above Monticello on the reverse
  • Wheat Pennies (1909–1958): Common but collectible. Key dates: 1909-S VDB, 1914-D, 1931-S (worth $50–$500+). All others worth a few cents to a few dollars
  • Silver Half Dollars: Franklin halves (1948–1963) are 90% silver. Kennedy halves (1965–1969) are 40% silver. 1970-D Kennedy halves are rare and worth searching for
  • Error Coins: Doubled dies, off-center strikes, wrong planchet errors. These require a loupe to identify and can be worth hundreds to thousands

How to Get Coin Rolls from the Bank

CRH YouTube channels frequently cover bank strategy — which banks will give you rolls, how many you can request, and whether to bring your own rolls to exchange. Key tips from top CRH creators:

  • Build relationships with tellers — regular customers get better service and more roll access
  • Half dollar rolls are the hardest to get but produce the highest silver hit rate — ask specifically for "customer-wrapped" half dollar rolls
  • Some banks no longer carry rolled coins — call ahead before visiting
  • Bring your found coins back to the same bank for exchange — it keeps the relationship positive
Starting your CRH journey: Watch 3–5 Treasure Town or CRH Hunting videos before your first roll search. You'll know exactly what to look for in each denomination and won't miss any silver hiding in plain sight. The key tool you need: a 10x loupe for examining dates and mint marks up close.

Frequently Asked Questions

Treasure Town is the most popular dedicated CRH channel with 400K+ YouTube subscribers, covering all roll types with clear explanations of what to look for. CRH Hunting is the best for strategy and educational content about CRH technique.
CRH is profitable if you're finding silver — each silver dime is worth ~$2, silver quarter ~$5, silver half dollar ~$10. Hit rates vary wildly by region. CRH YouTube channels frequently discuss expected hit rates and which denominations produce the most silver per dollar searched.
Focus on: Silver Roosevelt dimes (1946–1964), silver Washington quarters (pre-1965), war nickels (1942–1945), wheat pennies, silver Kennedy and Franklin half dollars, and error coins. CRH YouTube channels show you exactly how to identify each one in real time.
CRH requires zero upfront investment beyond your initial coin purchase — you get coins at face value from the bank and return what you don't keep at face value. A box of pennies costs $25, a box of halves costs $500. You'll need a 10x loupe ($10–$15 on Amazon) to properly examine dates and mint marks.
