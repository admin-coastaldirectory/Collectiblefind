Bullion & Precious Metals Stacking Channels

Bullion stacking channels cover the intersection of coin collecting and precious metals investing. They track silver and gold spot prices, review mint products, show off stack unboxings, and discuss long-term strategy for building a physical metals position. Silver Dragons is the dominant voice in this space, but a growing community of bullion-focused creators provides excellent market commentary and product reviews.

Ancient & World Coin Collecting Channels

Ancient coin collecting — covering Greek, Roman, Byzantine, and other historical coinage — is one of the fastest-growing corners of numismatics on YouTube. These channels cover coin cleaning techniques (a necessary skill for ancient coins), provenance research, authentication, and the historical context that makes ancient coins one of the most intellectually engaging collecting hobbies. World coin channels cover modern and historical coinage from outside the US, a broad and underserved niche with passionate collectors.

Coin Roll Hunting YouTube Channels

Coin roll hunting (CRH) is one of the most accessible entry points into coin collecting — you get rolls of coins from the bank, search for silver coins, wheat pennies, and error coins, then return the non-keepers. CRH channels on YouTube document these searches in real time, making for some of the most exciting content in the hobby. The best CRH channels combine entertainment value with genuine education about what to look for.

What to find CRH: Silver Roosevelt dimes (1946–1964), silver Washington quarters (pre-1965), war nickels (1942–1945), wheat pennies (1909–1958), and bicentennial quarters and half dollars are the most sought-after CRH finds. Top CRH YouTubers show you exactly how to identify each one.

Best Coin Collecting YouTube Channels for Beginners

If you're new to coin collecting, start with channels that prioritize education over entertainment. The best beginner-friendly numismatic YouTube channels cover: how to identify coin grades, how to use the Red Book (A Guide Book of US Coins), how to safely handle and store coins, what to buy first, and how to avoid common beginner mistakes like overcleaning coins.
